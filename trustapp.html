Why TrustApp

A central policy plane for direct-to-data apps and agents

Enterprises are building custom applications and AI agents that connect straight to business systems and APIs—prioritizing low latency and architectural simplicity. That direct path avoids proxy overhead, but raises a hard question: is the app allowed to make this call, and is the actor behind it allowed to see this resource? TrustApp answers both, at every request. Together with TrustAI, TrustLogix delivers a unified control plane across every data-access architecture.

The Challenge

Pitfalls of direct-to-data architecture

Many organizations deliberately avoid MCP or proxy architectures for production applications to gain speed and simplicity:

Lower latency Simpler architecture Fewer infrastructure components Direct integration with enterprise systems

Without a robust security and governance approach, however, these benefits come with real disadvantages:

Embedded Security

Developers embed rules that become difficult to audit, modify, and govern.

Fragmented Data Policies

Applications evolve independently, creating inconsistent policies across the enterprise.

No Centralized Visibility

No unified insight into who accessed what data and why.

Complex Policy Updates

Security and compliance changes require updates to application code.

How It Works

From access request to policy decision in four easy steps

TrustApp is a centralized policy decision point for enterprise applications. Every authorization request is evaluated against your central policies—consistent security across custom apps, AI agents, and enterprise data platforms.

Register

Connect applications using REST APIs and register the relevant protected resources.

Define Policies

Bring in users, apps, and resource attributes from IdPs like Entra or Okta, then author centralized authorization policies.

Evaluate in Real-time

Every application request is evaluated in real-time against centralized authorization policies.

Audit Every Decision

Log every request: who requested access, which app initiated it, the target resource, and why it was granted or denied.
